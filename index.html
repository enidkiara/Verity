Verity Safety Guide: Protect Yourself From Online Scams

Stay Safe From Online Scams

Scammers aren’t just sending random messages — they’re trying to:

  • Steal your identity
  • Gain access to your accounts
  • Trick you into sending money
  • Install malware on your device
  • Capture your login or verification codes

Understanding their goal helps you recognize the tactics they use.

Common Scam Tactics (and How They Work)

A. Urgency + Fear

Scammers create panic so you act without thinking.

  • “Your bank account will be closed in 24 hours.”
  • “We detected suspicious activity — verify now.”

Why it works: Fear overrides logic. Real companies rarely use threats.

B. Impersonation

They pretend to be:

  • Banks
  • Government agencies
  • Delivery services
  • Tech support
  • Friends or family

How they fake legitimacy:

  • Spoofed phone numbers
  • Fake email domains
  • Copied logos and formatting

C. Fake Links + Login Pages

These pages look identical to real websites but steal your credentials.

  • Extra characters in the URL
  • Misspelled domains
  • Shortened links (bit.ly, tinyurl)

D. Verification Code Theft

This is one of the fastest‑growing scam types.

  • Scammer tries to log into your account
  • You receive a real verification code
  • They ask you to “read it back”

Important: No legitimate company will ever ask for your 2FA code.

E. Unexpected Attachments

PDFs, ZIP files, or “invoices” that install malware.

Rule: If you didn’t expect it, don’t open it.

How to Check If a Message Is Legit

Step 1 — Look at the sender

  • Does the email domain match the real company
  • Is the phone number suspicious
  • Does the name look slightly off

Step 2 — Read the tone

  • Is it threatening
  • Is it overly urgent
  • Is it asking for personal info

Step 3 — Inspect the links

Hover over them (don’t click) and check the real URL.

Step 4 — Ask yourself: Did I initiate this

Real companies rarely contact you out of nowhere.

What To Do If You're Unsure

  • Don’t click anything
  • Don’t reply
  • Don’t share codes or passwords
  • Go to the official website manually
  • Call the company using their real number
  • Paste the message into Verity for analysis

If something feels off, trust that feeling.

If You Think You’ve Been Scammed

Immediate Actions

  • Change your passwords
  • Enable two‑factor authentication
  • Log out of all devices
  • Freeze your credit card if needed

Check for Damage

  • Look for unknown logins
  • Review recent transactions
  • Check your email forwarding rules

Report It

  • Contact your bank
  • Notify the platform (PayPal, Amazon, Apple, etc.)
  • File a report with your local fraud center

Long‑Term Safety Habits

  • Use strong, unique passwords
  • Turn on 2FA everywhere
  • Keep your device updated
  • Avoid public Wi‑Fi for sensitive accounts
  • Never reuse passwords
  • Store recovery codes safely

Small habits = big protection.

Common Scam Examples

Fake Delivery Message

“Your package is on hold. Click the link below to confirm your address.”

  • Uses urgency
  • Contains suspicious link
  • Pretends to be a delivery service

Account Suspension Email

“We noticed unusual activity. Verify your account to avoid suspension.”

  • Threatens account loss
  • Requests login verification

Legitimate Message

“Your appointment is confirmed for Tuesday at 3pm.”

  • No links
  • No personal info requested
  • Neutral language
